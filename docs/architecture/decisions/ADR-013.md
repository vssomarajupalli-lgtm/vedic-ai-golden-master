# ADR-013: Formula Final State Supremacy (Strict Tone-Locking)

## Status

## Context
As the system integrated the Answer Composer (LLM), there was a risk that the LLM might hallucinate or alter the deterministic outcome of the astrological engine based on its own internal weights or prompt phrasing.

## Repository Evidence
"Strict System Prompting must be enforced in the templates to mandate that the Formula's `FINAL STATE` is absolute law."

## Decision
The pre-calculated mathematical output (the Final State) from the Formula must dictate the absolute tone and answer for the LLM.

## Architectural Rationale
This prevents LLM hallucinations where prompt phrasing or generative tendencies might override the actual mathematical calculations produced by the astrological engines.

## Consequences
The Answer Composer is strictly tone-locked. It cannot alter the `FINAL STATE` determined by the formula, preserving the deterministic nature of the prediction.

## Compliance Notes
The LLM acts purely as a formatting tool and must be strictly bounded to enforce the Final State. Any logic that allows the LLM to evaluate or override the final outcome is an architectural violation.
